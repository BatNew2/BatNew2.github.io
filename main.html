    Benefits and Downsides of Renewable Energy

    Benefits of Renewable Energy

    Generating energy that produces no greenhouse gas emissions from fossil fuels and reduces some types of air pollution. Switching to clean sources of energy, such as wind, solar, hydro, and geothermal helps address not only climate change but also air pollution and health. The main reason why we should move to renewable energy is because of the perks and advantages it gives.

    A Fuel Supply That Never Runs Out

    Renewable Energy provides energy that naturally replenish themselves.

    Zero Carbon Emissions

    Renewable Energy doesn't create greenhouse gasses/pollutants.

    A Cheaper Form Of Electricity

    Renewable Energy becomes a very cheap source to generate electricity.

    Renewable Energy Creates New Jobs

    Renewable Energy provides more employment slots.

    Higher Capital Costs

    The cost to build renewable energy comes at a price.

    Electricity Production Can Be Unreliable

    Renewable energy rely on natural resources and weather, thus very unpredictable.

    Impacted by Enviromental Conditions

    Renewable Energy depends on the location and surrounding environment.

    Creates Carbon Footprint

    Although it doesn't make carbon emissions, it still creates a carbon footprint.

    Downsides of Renewable Energy

    While renewable energy offers numerous benefits, it also has some downsides. Technologies like solar, wind, and hydro depend on weather conditions or geographic availability, making them less consistent without proper storage systems. Additionally, high upfront costs and potential environmental impacts, such as habitat disruption, can pose challenges to widespread adoption.



    Why Indonesia?

    According to statistics from IRENA(The International Renewable Energy Agency), Indonesia only produces 1.6% of Asia's total renewable energy production in Asia (2023) despite favorful conditions that can be taken advantage of. This highlights a significant gap between Indonesia's potential and its current renewable energy output. With abundant natural resources such as solar, wind, geothermal, and hydropower, the country has the capacity to become a regional leader in renewable energy production.
